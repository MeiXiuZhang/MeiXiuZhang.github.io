Background on me, Mei Xiu Amy Zhang

I am a current freshman at Barnard College,
studying CS (Computer Science) and perhaps minoring in economics
(in the spirit of our sell out culture). I am originally from New
York City and am happily proud to be a first generation, low income
student. I come from a family of five and am the youngest of three
siblings (born to immigrant parents). As such, I am the most talkative,
loudest and most annoying of the three. I have always been short and
have always studied hard in school, willing to wake up at 5 AM on weekday
mornings and arriving at school before 7:30 AM. Being unable to depend
on my father, I saw myself grow up in many ways: I became outspoken
when someone made racial slurs against my culture, I became comfortable
with public transportation and I became the chaperone at doctor appointments.
One of my favorite memories from high school was Girls Who Code, a company
whose summer immersion program showed me that women can pursue their dreams
and that STEM is a place for women. Through this program, I learned
about coding, mentorship and my desire to be an empowering young women.
Although my parents are still worried about a girl going into
a male-dominated field, I've learned not to let critiques or
fear stop me. And here I am now, well on my way to creating a tradition
of proud, Barnard graduates in the Zhang family.

Life as a Barnard Freshman

Current Me

I take 8:40 classes almost everyday of the week and am
constantly in meetings for my favorite time commitment, the "tech frat of Columbia University" ADI.
I am also on the Student Government Association at Barnard
as a Class Representative and also have weekly meetings for that.
More than ever, I am passionate to be a woman pursuing CS and meeting mentors
has made me want to become one in the ever growing STEM field.
In fact, I am a coordinator for the CS Mentorship program because I
want to match more freshmen/sophomores to more experienced students that they can
help them in many differnt ways (especially outside of just
academics). I don't procrastinate so find me in my room, writing another
essay or coding for actual class or for personal interest. I actually
sleep a lot more than I did in high school and *gasp* socialize.
TL;DR: Stress culture is real but it's a fun time :)
