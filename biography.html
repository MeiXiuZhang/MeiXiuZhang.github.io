Background on me, Mei Xiu Amy Zhang

I am a current freshman at Barnard College, studying CS (Computer Science) and perhaps minoring in economics (in the spirit of our sell out culture). I am originally from New York City and am happily proud to be a first generation, low income student. I come from a family of five and am the youngest of three siblings (born to immigrant parents). As such, I am the most talkative, loudest and most annoying of the three. As for my history, I have always been short and have always studied hard in school, willing to wake up at 5 AM on weekday mornings and arriving at school before 7:30 AM. Being unable to depend on my father, I saw myself grow up in many ways: I became outspoken when someone made racial slurs against my culture, I became comfortable with public transportation and I became the chaperone at doctor appointments. One of my favorite memories from high school was Girls Who Code, a company whose summer immersion program showed me that women can pursue their dreams and that STEM is a place for women.
